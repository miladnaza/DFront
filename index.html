Information

Our group, consisting of Milad, Zain, Cherrie, and Gogo, collaborated to develop a comprehensive SQL database for managing various aspects of flight operations. This database was designed to efficiently handle flight schedules, airplane details, pilot assignments, passenger bookings, and luggage management. Through interconnected tables like `flight`, `airplane`, `location`, `passenger`, and `ticket`, we created a robust system to ensure data accuracy and consistency. Advanced SQL features such as constraints, triggers, and sequences automated processes like ID generation, data validation, and event logging, reducing the risk of errors and manual work. Additionally, we incorporated functions and procedures to enhance functionality and performance. For example, a geospatial calculation function determines flight durations based on origin and destination, while a procedure automates flight data insertion by calculating arrival times. Triggers were implemented to log updates, ensuring transparency and tracking of key changes. Throughout the project, we worked collaboratively to refine the database design, ensuring it was scalable and capable of meeting the complex demands of a flight management system. This project reflects our combined efforts and showcases our ability to design, implement, and optimize a real-world database solution.
